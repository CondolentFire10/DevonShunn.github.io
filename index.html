// WHO I AM

Not Just an Engineer.
A Builder.

I've been taking things apart and putting them back together — usually better than I found them — for as long as I can remember. What started with Lego Technic sets and a curiosity about why things worked the way they did has turned into a full-stack approach to making: hardware, software, fabrication, and the code that ties them all together.

I'm currently studying engineering, but the honest truth is that most of what I know came from building things at 1am and figuring out why they broke. That's not a complaint — it's the best education I've found. Failing a print, debugging a state machine, resolving a servo linkage conflict in CAD — that's where understanding actually lives.

My work spans embedded systems, 3D design and fabrication, interactive software, and increasingly the intersection of all three. I care a lot about craft — not just whether something works, but whether it's built right. Clean wire routing, tight tolerances, code that doesn't block itself. The details no one sees unless they're looking for them.

Outside the build desk: I'm a huge Star Wars fan (the lightsaber and BD-1 aren't accidents), a sim racing enthusiast who decided building the wheel was more interesting than buying one, and someone who will absolutely spend three weekends perfecting a paint weathering technique before calling something done.
