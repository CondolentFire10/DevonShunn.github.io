// WHO I AM

Not Just an Engineer.
A Builder.

I've been taking things apart and putting them back together, usually better than I found them, for as long as I can remember. What started with Lego Technic sets and a curiosity about how things work has turned into a full-stack approach to creating: hardware, software, fabrication, and the code that ties it all together.

I'm currently pursuing degrees in engineering and economics, but most of what I've learned has come from late-night projects — designing, troubleshooting, and rebuilding until something finally works. And this isn’t a complaint. It’s honestly the most effective way to learn that I’ve found. A failed print job, debugging a state machine, resolving a servo linkage conflict in CAD: that’s where understanding actually lives.

Most of my projects live where embedded systems, mechanical design, and interactive software overlap. I care a lot about craft, not just whether something works, but whether it’s built right. Clean wire routing, tight tolerances, code that doesn’t block itself. The details no one sees unless they’re looking for them.

Outside the build desk, I’m a huge Star Wars fan (the lightsaber and BD-1 aren’t by accident), a sim racing enthusiast who decided building the wheel was more interesting than buying one, and someone who will absolutely spend three weekends perfecting a paint weathering technique before calling something done.
